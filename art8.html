Painter = Vincent Van Gogh

Starry nights over the Rhone’, captures the reflections of the gaslighting in Arles across the glimmering blue water of the Rhone. Along with two lovers in the foreground, he shows his stars’ glow with a luminescence, shining from the dark, blue, and velvety night sky. The spot was only a minute or two’s walk from The Yellow House, which Van Gogh was renting at the time. Van Gogh was genuinely moved by this night scene and the experience of the endless darkness on the bank of the river,  Which he described in a letter: “Once I went for a walk along the deserted shore at night. It was not cheerful; it was not sad – it was beautiful.” This famous painting hangs in the Musée d’Orsay, Paris, France.
